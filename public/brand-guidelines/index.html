11 / Compliance & Disclaimers

Required disclaimer language

Use this exact language. RUO labeling is a strict regulatory fence — the FDA treats website content, marketing, and reviews as the primary enforcement trigger, so the rules below are not optional.

1 · Website homepage & footer disclaimer

Place in the footer of every page and prominently on the homepage:

"The products on this website are intended for research, laboratory, and in vitro use only. They are not intended for human consumption or use in products that come into contact with humans. By browsing this site, you acknowledge that you are a qualified researcher, student, or laboratory technician. These products are not drugs, supplements, or cosmetics, and have not been evaluated or approved by the FDA."

2 · Product labeling & packaging

Every vial, carton, and outer box must explicitly state:

For Research Use Only. Not for human or animal use.
  • Five required elements — product name, net quantity, lot/batch number, expiration date, and the RUO statement.
  • COA per lot — batch-specific Certificate of Analysis with HPLC/identity data tied to the lot number.
  • Every level — the RUO notice appears on the vial, the inner carton, the outer shipping box, and the shipment MSDS/SDS and packing slip.

3 · The FDA "compliance rule"

A disclaimer alone does not protect a seller if the surrounding marketing implies human use. To avoid warning letters or product seizure:

  • No therapeutic claims — do not market for weight loss, muscle building, anti-aging, or disease mitigation.
  • No dosage guidance — no instructions, reconstitution guides, or suggestions for human application.
  • No testimonials — remove or avoid before/after photos or reviews mentioning personal health results.

Further guidance: FDA — "Telehealth Companies: What to Know When Promoting Compounded Drugs" (fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding).

12 / Marketing Guidelines Checklist

Run every asset through this

Before any ad, post, email, product page, or label goes live, check every box. One miss is an enforcement trigger.

Always do

  • ☐ RUO statement present — "For Research Use Only. Not for human or animal use." on the asset or page.
  • ☐ Research framing only — describe in-vitro / laboratory / assay applications, nothing else.
  • ☐ Qualified-audience language — speak to researchers, students, lab technicians.
  • ☐ COA available — link or reference the batch COA on every product.
  • ☐ Homepage + footer disclaimer live — on every page.
  • ☐ Compliance partner sign-off — label and packaging artwork reviewed before print.

Never do

  • ☐ No therapeutic / health claims — weight loss, muscle, anti-aging, healing, disease.
  • ☐ No dosing or human-use instructions — no reconstitution-for-injection, no protocols.
  • ☐ No testimonials or before/after — no personal-results reviews, no transformation photos.
  • ☐ No human imagery implying use — no people injecting, applying, or consuming product.
  • ☐ No "supplement / drug / cure" wording — these are not drugs, supplements, or cosmetics.
  • ☐ No linking to human-use content — blogs, influencers, or sites that imply human use.
